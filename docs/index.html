The point. Bus operators across the West of England publish their live vehicle data, and WECA's Bus Service Improvement Plan sets a 95% punctuality target for the area, but there is no accessible, route-level public scorecard showing whether that target is being met. This site builds one from the operators' own open data.
What we claim
  • These figures are our independent measurement of on-time performance at timing points, computed from operators' public BODS real-time feeds against the published timetable.
  • We use the DfT statistical on-time definition: a stop counts as on-time if the bus is between 1 minute early and 5 min 59 s late.
  • The method, the code, and the limitations are all open. Anyone can reproduce or challenge them.
What we do not claim
  • We do not claim any operator is breaking the law or withholding required data. Operators are legally required to publish timetables, vehicle locations and fares, and they do. Performance figures are not a statutory publication requirement.
  • We do not claim these are the official figures. They are an independent estimate and will differ from any internal operator or regulator measurement.
  • Low-sample routes, smaller operators with patchier real-time data, and partial collection days are not reliable on their own, and are flagged as such.
Full methodology

The complete method, data sources (including the routes drawn from TNDS that operators do not publish to BODS), the geography, fleet and frequent-service definitions, and every limitation are documented in AUDIT_METHODOLOGY.md in the GitHub repository.
